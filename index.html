About

I’m a Ph.D. candidate in Computational and Integrative Biology at Rutgers University–Camden, where my research bridges machine learning, bioinformatics, and neuroscience. My work focuses on developing generative and multimodal AI models for automated tissue image and multi-omics data analysis to uncover deeper biological insights.

Prior to graduate school, I spent two years in industry as a Machine Learning Engineer at Apurba Technologies Inc. and CMED Health Ltd., developing large-scale OCR and healthcare analytics systems. I’m passionate about translating AI innovations into tools that deepen our understanding of biology and improve human health.

Outside of research, I enjoy playing cricket and volleyball and exploring new ideas through reading, travel, and meaningful conversations.
